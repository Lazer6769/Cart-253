Visually

CCTVs are a prominent form of visual surveillance in our society. It keeps track of our every move and is there for “security purposes”. We use these cameras to find people, see where they last were, and what they look like. We also use them to regulate the behaviour in our society. The cameras are an extension of the panopticon’s principles, where someone is watching your every move, and you have to regulate your behaviour to fit into society's expectations and not get in trouble and face unforeseen consequences.

Phone cameras (facial scanning): Facial scanning is both a good and a bad issue that is rising in our society. At first, it seems good to have your face unlock your phone, which is great to ensure that no one else is able to use it—scanning people’s faces to see who’s done what and who hasn't. But the issue with facial scanning is that our faces are being scanned 24/7, which gives access to our visual appearance being stolen. It steals who we are and exploits our faces for the use of those who rule

Hidden cameras (Ring cameras): Hidden cameras like the Ring cameras can be useful. It helps us view our front porch, seeing who is there and who isn’t. However, while the ring camera isn't so hidden actual hidden camera can be used for sinister purposes. It allows the person who is using them to watch our every move without us noticing, and it allows them to use the footage and sell it online without our knowledge. While we think we're safe in our home, someone can be watching us within our home
